# Prompt: Social Post (LinkedIn / X)

## Editorial Voice

Same as the blog — a practitioner with 20 years in enterprise software, Matt Levine register.

On social, the wisdom has to land faster. One insight per post, earned by experience, stated plainly. The reader should feel like they ran into someone who's been doing this for two decades and just told them something they'll repeat at their next stand-up.

Social-specific rules:

- The hook names a pattern, not a product. Something the reader has lived through.
- "We've seen this" is the implicit authority behind every claim. Sometimes you say it explicitly.
- No hashtag spam. No emoji unless the irony earns it. No "Excited to share."
- One idea. Not five bullet points dressed up as one post.

## The Anti-Patterns We're Avoiding

- "We're thrilled to announce our revolutionary solution..."
- "In today's digital landscape..."
- Lists of 7 features nobody asked about
- Manufactured urgency ("Don't miss out")
- Humble bragging disguised as a lesson

## LinkedIn Post

```text
Write a LinkedIn post about: [TOPIC]

The practitioner angle:
- What pattern does this relate to that we've seen repeatedly over 20 years?
- What's the thing most people do wrong, and why?
- What does it actually look like when it works?

Format:
- Hook: a situation or observation the reader has been in. Specific, slightly ironic.
- 2–3 short paragraphs. Simple language, no jargon.
- One plain takeaway — the thing worth repeating.
- Max 2 hashtags, only if not embarrassing.

Tone: warm, dry, quietly confident. The reader should feel like they got a useful
piece of hard-won perspective, not a sales message.
```

## X / Twitter Thread

```text
Write a 3-tweet thread about: [TOPIC]

Tweet 1: the pattern — something that's always true about how this usually goes.
         Stated as observation, not complaint. Specific enough to be recognizable.
Tweet 2: what we've learned — the actual insight, concrete. If there's a number,
         a query, a decision rule — put it here.
Tweet 3: the honest implication or a quiet CTA. No "link in bio" energy.

Max 280 chars each. Each tweet should stand alone.
Cut anything that sounds like it was written by a marketing team.
```
