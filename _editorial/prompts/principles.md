# Prompt: Business Principles Post

## Format

A "principles" post is a short, standalone piece built around one hard-won business observation. Not advice, not a how-to — a pattern that keeps showing up after enough time in the field, stated plainly enough that the reader can test it against their own experience.

The principle is the headline. Everything else exists to make it land.

## Structure

1. **The principle** — stated as a title: short, direct, slightly uncomfortable. A declaration, not a question.
2. **The failure mode** — what happens when this principle is ignored. Specific scenario, present tense. One or two paragraphs. The reader should recognize the situation.
3. **Why it keeps happening** — the organizational logic that produces the failure. Not stupidity — usually a rational response to a wrong incentive or a gap in visibility.
4. **The corrective** — what following the principle actually looks like in practice. Concrete, not motivational.
5. **Callout** — one sentence that distills the principle for someone who only reads that line: `<div class="highlight">...</div>`

## Voice

Same practitioner register as newsroom posts, but tighter. No warm-up. Start with the situation. The reader should feel: *someone finally said this out loud.*

Length: 300–500 words.
